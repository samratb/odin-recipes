Chicken Biryani

Description

Kolkata Chicken Biryani is a spicy rice-based dish, a local tradition originating from Mughlai cuisine. The name of the dish comes from the city of Kolkata in West Bengal. Rice is used as the base ingredient, and meat, eggs, potatoes and spices are added. It is best known for its use of potatoes, and is famous for its light blend of spices.

Ingredients of Kolkata Chicken Biryani

  • 1200 gms chicken
  • 4 medium potatoes
  • 3 medium onions
  • 2 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 1/2 tsp ginger paste
  • 1/2 Lemon (juiced)
  • 1/2 cup curd
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 3 1/2 tsp biryani masala
  • 3 drops rose essence
  • 1 1/3 tsp kewra water
  • 8-9 drops mitha attar
  • 1 Kg Basmati rice or long grain rice
  • A pinch of saffron strand
  • 4 tbsp milk
  • 5 Bay leaves
  • 2-3 inch cinnamon stick
  • 8-10 Cloves
  • 10 Green cardamom or elaichi
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 10-12 tbsp ghee
  • 1/2 cup refined oilSalt (as required)
  • 6-7 Hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • 50 gms bay leaves (for spreading on the bottom)
  • 1 tsp shah jeera
  • 1 tsp white peppercorn
  • 25 green cardamom
  • 1 1/2 Mace or javitri
  • 1 1/2 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1/4 nutmeg
  • 1 tsp kebab chini or all spice
  • 5 Cloves

Steps

  1. Take medium to large size potatoes, wash and peel them. Cut into halves or quarters and cook until tender.
  2. Once the potatoes completely cool down, heat a pan with 1/2 cup of ghee and 1/2 cup of oil on medium flame, then fry the potatoes until golden brown.
  3. In the same pan, fry onions until crispy.
  4. Prepare Bengali biryani masala by dry roasting shahi jeera, white peppercorn (shah morich), cardamom, mace, cinnamon stick, nutmeg, kabab china, and cloves on a low flame until aromatic.
  5. Cool the spices and grind them into a powder.
  6. Clean and wash the chicken pieces (curry cut), ensuring they are large in size.
  7. In a bowl, mix 1/2 cup of curd, 1 tsp of kashmiri chilli powder, and 1/2 tsp of salt.
  8. Add the chicken pieces to the curd mixture, then add 2 tsp of garlic paste, 1 1/2 tsp of ginger paste, and mix well.
  9. Add 1 1/2 tsp of the prepared biryani masala powder and mix nicely.
  10. Squeeze the juice of 1/2 lemon into the mixture for marination.
  11. Add a handful of fried onions and mix together. Marinate for at least 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  12. After marination, place the marinated chicken in the pan used for frying onions and cook on medium flame.
  13. Cook for 5 minutes, then turn the pieces and cover the pan with a lid to cook for 10 minutes.
  14. Turn the chicken pieces again and cook for another 8-10 minutes without the lid until the gravy mostly dries up.
  15. Wash 1 kg of basmati or long grain rice gently and soak for at least 30 minutes.
  16. In a large vessel, bring 3/4 water to a boil. Add salt, refined oil, bay leaves, green cardamoms, cloves, and cinnamon sticks.
  17. Once the water boils, strain the soaked rice and add it to the vessel. Cook until the rice is 80 percent done.
  18. Immediately drain the starch and spread the rice on plates to cool down completely.
  19. Prepare saffron milk by soaking a pinch of saffron strands in warm milk for 15-20 minutes, then rubbing the strands to release more color.
  20. Add kewra water, 3 drops of rose essence, and 8-9 drops of mitha attar to the saffron milk mixture and mix well.
  21. To assemble, take a thick-bottomed vessel and spread bay leaves on the bottom.
  22. Spread a 1/2 to 3/4 inch thick layer of rice on the bay leaves.
  23. Place two chicken pieces and three pieces of potatoes on the first layer, then add 3-4 tbsp of chicken gravy.
  24. Sprinkle 1/2 tsp biryani masala, 3-4 tsp of the essence-added saffron milk, 2-3 tsp of ghee, a handful of crispy fried onion, and 1/2 tsp of salt over the layer.
  25. Add another layer of rice to completely cover the first layer.
  26. Assemble the next layer using the same process, then cover with white rice and make a third layer similarly.
  27. Cover the last layer with ample rice, then sprinkle 3-4 tsp of ghee, a pinch of biryani masala, and salt. Place hard-boiled eggs on top and close the lid.
  28. Put the biryani vessel on the lowest heat of the oven and place another pan full of hot water on top of the lid.
  29. Cook for 30-45 minutes or until the outer side and lid of the vessel are very hot to the touch.
  30. Turn off the heat. Kolkata special chicken biryani is ready to serve with a side dish or salad.
